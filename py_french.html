For about two years (2018-2019), I spent quite a bit of time learning French from zero.

I often found it challenging to remember the gender of different French nouns, especially when a noun ending in "e" turned out to be masculine (e.g. Lycée and école both end in "e". Lycée is masculine whereas école is feminine).

Wanting to brush up my Python a bit, I took the opportunity to write a program (GitHub repo) that I used to quiz myself on the gender of French nouns.

The program runs via command line. Below are the instructions upon initiation: 

$ python3 french.py 

* * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * *

Gender Quiz for Nouns!

'm' = nm
'f' = nf
'mf' = nm/nf
'mpl' = nmpl
'fpl' = nfpl
'm(pl)' = nm(pl)
'f(pl)' = nf(pl)
'mi' = nmi
'n' = n

Use ';' without space to separate multiple answers.

Answers should match meanings in order.

E.g.
merci
intj : thank you
NOUN : thank you (masculine)
NOUN : mercy (feminine)

Expected answers: 'm;f' (not 'f;m')
