My co-first-author paper on mouse anti-SARS-CoV-2 antibodies in the Journal of Immunology.

The code that I wrote for this project is publicly accessible on Bitbucket.


In this study, we profiled and examined B cells isolated from mice immunized against SARS-CoV-2, the causal agent of COVID-19.

First, we computationally inferred the clonal relationships of B cells of interest (left), using a distance threshold chosen with the aid of visualization (right).



Then, we showed that the B cells that were responsive to SARS-CoV-2 (RBD+) were homogeneous in terms of their gene expression as B cells that were non-responsive (RBD-) (left).

We also showed that the responsive B cells were significantly more mutated than the non-responsive ones (right).
