Starting as a statistics undergrad major and later as a PhD-level computational immunologist, I've been performing scientific computing and visualization for over 7 years now!

I absolutely love making pretty pictures that rely on sound visualization principles to promote open science.

So far, I've primarily been visualizing using R, but I would love to venture out into using more Python and JavaScript/D3 as well!

Feel free to check out some of my most recently published work in scientific journals. There's also a gallery with a selection of various types of visualizations I've made -- with the help of open-source libraries, of course!
